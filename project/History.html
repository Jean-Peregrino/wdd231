History and Origins

The Cajamarca Carnival, known as the "Happiest Festival in Peru," has its roots in pre-Hispanic and European traditions. It has been celebrated since colonial times and has evolved into a massive cultural event. The festival includes a mix of Andean rituals and Spanish customs, reflecting the region's rich cultural heritage.
